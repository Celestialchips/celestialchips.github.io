A Long time ago in a galaxy far, far away...

Episode I

A New Hope

Ryan Glass was born in Las Vegas, NV on October 28. He was the youngest of three, an older sister that was 4 years older, and an older brother that was 16 years older.

Hoping to resolve world issues with his engineering and problem solving mindset. As a young kid, his sister really raised him. We played school

As a child, he fell in love with the scientific method and started solving problems from a young age, his first engineering feat was a way to turn off his light because he was afraid of the dark. The contraption used a string tied to the light switch, a cabinet, and his favorite DragonBall Z Action Figure Goku.

Episode II

WINDOWS 98 OS

Ryan's first computer was a windows 98 OS, utilizing the power of AOL and a phone jack. Little did he know this would eventually fuel his love for computers.

One day the computer finally died, and needed to be replaced. So afterwards he took the computer out to the computer graveyard, Also known as the trash but before that happened. He wanted to see how a computer worked and what caused such a beautiful machine to tick as it did.

This was eventually how he figured out that a computer runs on electricity signals and decided to learn the parts that made a computer work. He saved each part in his room after taking the computer apart believing which parts were the most important.

Episode III

WINDOWS XP

The next OS that Ryan could experiement and play on was Windows XP. There were many new toys and evolutions more games were out. Red Alert, Star Craft, Diablo II, Lands of Lore III, Pharoah. This was also during the evolution of Flash Games. The internet was getting big.

Episode IV

WINDOWS VISTA

Windows Vista was the next iteration, the computer that Ryan was using was a Sony VAIO laptop that was able to run flash games and 2D side scrollers such as GunBound, and MapleStory. It wasn't much as his love for computers were still strong.

The laptop eventually stopped working due to Ryan's addiction to torrenting music and most likely all of the viruses that accompanied those downloads.

Episode IV

ASUS RoG

The first real computer experience Ryan had was an ASUS ROG gaming computer. The computer contained windows Vista, later being updated to Windows 7. This is where Ryan was able to play games such as Call of Duty Modern Warface 2. Things were never going to be the same after this era.

Episode V

First Developments

A period of learning, Ryan while playing a game he loved, he learned how to edit and open game files. Which was simple work, most of the files were actually stored onto the client computer.

Once learning how to modify in game skins to have custom skins(client sided), he moved onto learning how to modify other artwork such as maps, player highlighting, and gun modifications, and many more.

The game itself was a pretty complicated piece of technology, that was utilizing another piece of complicated technology Valve Anti-Cheat System (VAC). Getting around VAC was pretty simple in the early days. But as the software became more complicated and cloud computing started to take rise. It became more and more tedious to get around Valve's system.

Ultimately he learned how to modify and host his own game servers creating new fun game modes, such as Gun Game and Predator vs Alien. Ultimately he recieved a ban after a few years of doing these game modifications. Because it was against Valve's ToS.

Episode VI

Continual Development

The experience he had during his time of playing Call of Duty, he learned a lot. Fueling his love for computers. Today you can find him hacking his everyday office work. Either by automating an easy task or figuring out solutions to automate others workload.

Episode VI

Hobbies

Ryan doesn't have many hobbies. He does like looking into financial news, reading life books, learning about mental health such as living with ADHD, programming, and snowboarding. He stopped playing video games when he realized that it felt like he might've had an addiction. Maybe one day when he feels more stable he will return to the world of video gaming.
